Book Reviews

Yup book reviews, we all love books, all types of books. However the only books we will be delving into are fantasy books from authors such as George R. R. Martin, Brandon Sanderson and Scott Lynch.

The Lies of Locke Lamora

The Lies of Locke Lamora, a book by Scott Lynch, is a story based in a fantasy world about the life of Locke Lamora, an oprhaned child. However the book does not delve into it's fantasy roots until the next book in the series, but we're just keeping the review to this one book.

We go through Locke Lamora's child hood via flash backs through out the book. Locke is part of a group of Elite con artists calling themselves the "Gentleman Bastards" rob the rich of the city of Camorr. In this way it's kind of like a heist book, but the way it's written with twists and turns, the plot really excels compared to many other books I've read.

All in all the book is a great read however as the series progresses the books start going down hill. I think this is due to Lynch trying to expand and build the world rather than just focusing on story and plot, his bread and butter. This first book would make a great stand alone though.

Around The World In 80 Days

About a distinguished gentleman who is apart of a club in which he makes a bet that he can travel the globe in 80 days. This is an old victorian novel written in 1873 by Jules Verne, which follows A man on his journey around the world (oobviously). A classic adventure novel suitable for those of all ages. I actually read this on a 5 hour train journey all in one sitting! that's how engrossing and fun it was.

A short to med-sized read Verne bring you accross the world with a character called Phileas Fogg. Though it is not from the aspect of Fogg himself, but his companions and friends he makes a long the way. From deep inside the Indian jungles, over the seas and oceans and all the way Through America, the story is gripping and will always keep you on your toes.

The Stormlight Archives

The Storm Light Archives is a huge series in the making written by Brandon Sanderson. Set in a huge fantasy world whcih is fantastically built by Sanderson. These books follow the Knights of Radiance as they seek to rebuild their establishment after it being destroyed a long while ago.

Each knight has their own set of powers and wonderous abilities all with thrilling and compelling story lines. Sanderson really know how to build a world and characters within it. The books are rather long and the series is set to be 10 books long. Each book has come near to or over 1000 words, so you know there is a lot of this world to soak up, if you hae the time that is.
